The Story

A living garden
behind an old wooden door

Hidden within the ancient walls of Mağusa, Mystery Garden is exactly what the name promises — and more. The stone building is old, the garden is serene and lush, and the family who runs it has been collecting beautiful things for a very long time. Kilim sofas, hanging gourds, Turkish ceramic plates, ornate ironwork.

"Highly rated for the garden, breakfast, and attentive service."

Guests enjoy a lounge, outdoor seating area, and a patio with garden views. Rooms have private bathrooms with walk-in showers, air conditioning, and tiled floors. And a continental breakfast with local specialities and cheese — served in-room — that will take longer than you planned.

After Dark

The courtyard comes
alive at night

By day the garden is lush and shaded, alive with plants that have been growing here for decades. By evening, when the lanterns come on and the city quietens outside the walls, it becomes something else entirely.

Palms, hanging lights, stone arches lit from below. The kind of place that makes you want to sit still and say nothing for a while.

Common Spaces

A room full of things
that tell a story

The sitting room is not designed — it is accumulated. Red kilim sofas, hanging gourds, a samovar, photographs, lanterns, and a hundred objects collected over a lifetime. It opens onto the courtyard garden, so the line between inside and outside blurs.

This is where you will have your morning coffee. This is where you will stay longer than you meant to.

of the old city
